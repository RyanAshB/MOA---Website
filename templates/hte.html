Step By Step: How To EXPORT

Plants & Plant Products

Export permits are required to validate the phytosanitary health of the product and its authenticity as an originating product from Jamaica. The primary agency that manages the export of plants and plant products is the Plant Quarantine and Produce Inspection (PQPI) division.

Export services are also provided by other agencies depending on the nature of the product. In addition to the PQPI, consult the agencies below for guidance if exporting Coffee, Cocoa, Coconut, Tumeric, Ginger, Other Spices, or Banana.

Step 1: Acquire Import Requirements

  • Acquire import requirements from importer in overseas market.

Step 2: Packing House Facility.

  • Utilizing Personal Packing House Facility.
  • Utilizing Packing House Facility From Another Exporter.

Step 3: Make Appointment With PQPI

  • Make an appointment for the inspection of the packing house by a Plant Quarantine Inspector.
  • If the packing house is approved, the following information must be provided:
    1. Name and address of company.
    2. Location of packing house.
    3. Name, address and telephone number of contact person.
  • If packing house is approved, the Chief Plant Quarantine Officer will prepare a letter to the Export Certification Officer, JAMPRO, requesting that they proceed with the certification process. The Produce may either be transported to the PQPI facility or the PQPI may also visit the exporter.
  • All produce for export must be prepared and packed in an approved packing house.
  • All produce must be appropriately packaged in new, clean, properly labelled bags or boxes.
  • Labelling on packages must coincide with the information on the export documents.
  • A Plant Quarantine/Produce Inspector must inspect all shipments. If the shipment is approved, an export certificate will be issued.
  • Appointments for inspection must be made 24 hours in advance before the shipment is brought to the export complex or before the time when the inspector is to inspect the shipment at the packing.

Use of Packing House Facility by Another Produce Exporter

As of March 1, 1999 the following conditions apply when you give permission to someone to use your packing house for the purpose of preparing and packaging produce for export:

  1. The arrangements should be for a period not exceeding six (6) months.
  2. A letter written on your companyâ€™s stationery and signed by you or one other person designated by you should accompany each shipment verifying that the produce was packed at your premises and also indicating the quantity of each item included in the shipment.
  3. The names of the two persons authorized to sign and a sample of their signature should be sent to the Plant Quarantine/Produce Inspection Division prior to the start of exportation.
  4. You will be required to notify the Plant Quarantine Office when you have ceased packing on behalf of the person.
  5. Please note that shipments will not be certified unless condition #2 is met each time a shipment is presented for inspection and certification.
